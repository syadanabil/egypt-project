About the Museum

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo, also known as the Museum of Egyptian Antiquities, is home to an extensive collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts dating back thousands of years. Founded in 1858 by French archaeologist Auguste Mariette, the museum houses over 120,000 items, with a representative amount on display.

Located in Tahrir Square in downtown Cairo, the museum's distinctive pink building was constructed in 1900 in the neoclassical style. It offers visitors an unparalleled journey through Egypt's fascinating history, from the prehistoric period through the Pharaonic era to the Greco-Roman period.

The museum's most famous exhibit is the treasures of Tutankhamun, including his iconic gold mask. Other highlights include the Royal Mummy Room, the Narmer Palette, and countless statues, jewelry, and artifacts that tell the story of one of the world's greatest ancient civilizations.

Famous Treasures

Tutankhamun's Gold Mask

The funerary mask of Tutankhamun is perhaps the most famous artifact from ancient Egypt. Discovered by Howard Carter in 1925, the mask is made of solid gold and inlaid with semi-precious stones.

Narmer Palette

Dating from about the 31st century BC, the Narmer Palette contains some of the earliest hieroglyphic inscriptions ever found. It is thought to depict the unification of Upper and Lower Egypt.

Statue of Khafre

This magnificent diorite statue depicts Pharaoh Khafre, builder of the second pyramid at Giza. The statue was discovered in his valley temple in 1860 and is a masterpiece of Old Kingdom art.

Royal Mummies

Ramses II

Often called Ramses the Great, he was the third pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty of Egypt. He is often regarded as the most celebrated and powerful pharaoh of the New Kingdom.

Hatshepsut

Hatshepsut was the fifth pharaoh of the Eighteenth Dynasty of Egypt. She was the second historically confirmed female pharaoh, and known for her successful reign and building projects.

Thutmose III

Thutmose III was the sixth pharaoh of the Eighteenth Dynasty. He created the largest empire Egypt had ever seen, with no fewer than 17 campaigns.

Seti I

Seti I was a pharaoh of the New Kingdom Nineteenth Dynasty. He was the father of Ramesses II and known for his military campaigns and building projects.
